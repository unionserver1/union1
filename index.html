A man arose and forged a kingdom. It flourished—vast beyond his will to bear. He left it, for he could not destroy The crown that chained him there. The people longed for another king. One rose, and with his rise came weight. He bargained strength for loyalty— Made dukes of men, and sealed his fate. The dukes grew proud, their hearts divine; They turned their power on their lord. The king struck back, but fell in time, And chaos claimed his sword. The kingdom burned beneath their strife, Its final king, with trembling hand, Undid his work to save the land— And silence swallowed life. Yet from the ashes, hope returned; A new crown glimmered, bright, forlorn. And just as before, the circle turned— A man arose, and a kingdom was born. - The Southern Kingdom Poem by Spade
